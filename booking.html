Phase 1: Research & Vetting

✅ Green Flags to Look For

  • Current portfolio photos

    Recent work, multiple angles, natural lighting

  • Detailed reviews (4.5+ stars)

    Specific comments about cuts, color, service

  • Professional certifications

    Brand training, continuing education mentioned

  • Clear pricing information

    No hidden fees, consultation policies stated

  • Active social media

    Regular posts, client interactions, before/afters

  • Clean, organized salon

    Professional photos, modern equipment visible

🚩 Red Flags to Avoid

  • Stock photos only

    No actual client work shown

  • Recent negative reviews

    Especially about damage or poor communication

  • Pressure to book immediately

    "Today only" deals, no consultation offered

  • Vague or missing pricing

    "Call for pricing" on basic services

  • Outdated website/photos

    2020 or older photos, broken links

  • No proper licensing

    Can't verify stylist credentials

Phase 2: The Essential Questions

📞 Questions for Initial Call

  1. 1
    "How long have you been cutting/coloring hair?"

    Look for 3+ years minimum, 10+ years ideal

  2. 2
    "What training do you have with [specific brand/technique]?"

    For balayage, extensions, specific color lines

  3. 3
    "Do you offer consultations?"

    Should be YES for major changes

  4. 4
    "What products do you use and why?"

    Should know benefits of their chosen lines

  5. 5
    "What's your policy if I'm not happy?"

    Should offer corrections within reason

💬 Questions for Consultation

  1. 1
    "What condition is my hair in currently?"

    They should assess before recommending

  2. 2
    "How will you achieve this look?"

    Should explain process step-by-step

  3. 3
    "How long will this take?"

    Realistic timeframe, not rushed

  4. 4
    "What's the maintenance schedule?"

    Honest about upkeep requirements

  5. 5
    "What's the total investment?"

    Include all costs: service + products + maintenance

🎯 Perfect Answer Examples

Good Answer:

"I've been specializing in balayage for 8 years. I'm certified through Goldwell and take continuing education classes quarterly. For your hair, I'd recommend..."

Bad Answer:

"I've done lots of hair. I can do whatever you want. It'll look great, don't worry."

Phase 3: Booking Smart

First Visit Strategy

  • Start with a trim or basic service
  • Test communication and technique
  • Save major changes for visit 2
  • Observe salon cleanliness
  • Note how they treat other clients

Best Booking Times

  • Tuesday-Thursday: Best availability
  • 10am-2pm: Stylist fresh & focused
  • Avoid Mondays: Often rushed
  • Avoid Friday evenings: Rushed for weekend
  • Book 2-4 weeks ahead: For quality stylists

Preparation Tips

  • • Bring inspiration photos
  • • List current products used
  • • Note hair history (color, treatments)
  • • Set realistic expectations
  • • Plan for extra time

Understanding Salon Pricing

💰 What Affects Pricing

Higher Prices Often Mean:

  • • Premium location (Atlantic Ave)
  • • Advanced training & certifications
  • • High-end products (Olaplex, K18)
  • • Longer appointment times
  • • Individual attention
  • • Better correction policies

Lower Prices May Indicate:

  • • New stylist building clientele
  • • Residential location
  • • Basic product lines
  • • Faster turnover
  • • Limited specialized services
