What is Balayage?

Balayage (pronounced bah-lee-AHZH) is a French word meaning "to sweep." Unlike traditional foil highlights that create uniform stripes, balayage involves hand-painting color onto the hair for a natural, graduated effect.

The result? Hair that looks like you've spent the summer at the beach - lighter at the ends and around the face, with subtle dimension throughout.

What Makes a Great Balayage Artist?

1. Hand-Painting Mastery

Balayage is an art form. Look for colorists who specialize in freehand techniques and have portfolios showing their work on different hair types.

2. Color Theory Knowledge

Great balayage requires understanding undertones, how colors lift, and how to neutralize brassiness. Ask about their experience with your specific hair color.

3. Consultation Skills

A good colorist will spend time understanding what you want, assess your hair's condition, and set realistic expectations.
