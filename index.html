After years of trial and error with South Florida salons, here's what actually matters when you're looking for someone to trust with your hair.

What great color work looks like

Let's be honest: finding a good colorist in South Florida isn't easy. The humidity alone makes everything harder, and there's no shortage of salons that overpromise and underdeliver.

What Actually Matters

I've sat in a lot of salon chairs over the years. Some great, many forgettable, a few I'd rather not think about. Here's what I've learned to look for:

Someone Who Listens First

The best stylists spend more time asking questions than talking about themselves. They want to understand your hair history, your lifestyle, what hasn't worked before.

Honest About What's Possible

Going from dark brown to platinum blonde in one session? A good colorist will tell you why that's a bad idea and map out a realistic plan instead.

Understands Florida Hair

Our humidity, sun exposure, and pool/beach lifestyle require specific knowledge. What works in New York doesn't always work here.

Consistent Results

Anyone can get lucky once. Look at their body of work over time. Do their clients look great months later, or just on Instagram day one?
